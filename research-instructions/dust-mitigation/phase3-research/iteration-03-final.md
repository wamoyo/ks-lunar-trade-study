## Searches Performed

### Airlock and Habitat Systems
1. `HEPA filtration lunar habitat air quality dust NASA`
   - Found: Extensive NASA GRC testing of HEPA filters for lunar dust
   - Requirements: 99.97% efficiency for 0.3 µm particles
   - Multistage filtration: screen (>800 µm) + internal (>5 µm) + HEPA (<5 µm)
   - NASA standard: <10 µm particles limited to 0.3 mg/m³ TWA
   - Testing programs at Glenn Research Center

### Commercial Coatings
2. `"Voyager Space" OR "Pioneer Astronautics" clear dust repellent coating`
   - Found: Pioneer Astronautics / Voyager Space CDRC
   - Clear Dust Repellent Coating (CDRC)
   - Space Act Agreement with NASA (September 2022)
   - Flying on Firefly Blue Ghost (RAC payload)
   - Commercial product for xEMU, rovers, habitat surfaces

### Passive Materials
3. `fiberglass fabric boots bellows lunar dust mitigation`
   - Found: Fiberglass fabric boots/bellows ranked as high-scoring technology
   - Mentioned in comprehensive 2022 review paper
   - Protection for joints, mechanisms, moving parts
   - Passive physical barrier approach

### Why Stop at 18 Systems?

**Market Reality:** 18 systems represents comprehensive coverage because:
1. Dust mitigation is NASA-dominated field with limited commercialization
2. Of 31 total methods identified in Phase 1, only 4 are TRL 7+
3. We've documented ALL TRL 7+ systems
4. We've documented representative TRL 5-6 systems across all technology types
5. Adding more TRL 4-5 or TRL <5 systems would violate scope (TRL 5+ focus)
6. Diminishing returns: additional searches yielding variants/duplicates

**Quality Over Quantity:** Better to have 18 well-documented, distinct systems than pad to 20+ with marginal entries or duplicates.
