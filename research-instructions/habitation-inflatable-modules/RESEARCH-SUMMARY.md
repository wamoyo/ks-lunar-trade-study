## Executive Summary

Research into lunar habitation and inflatable modules has identified **10 primary vendors/systems** across four technology categories: inflatable/expandable modules, rigid pressurized structures, 3D printed in-situ construction, and life support integration. Technology Readiness Levels range from TRL 4 (early development) to TRL 9 (operational), with most systems at TRL 5-7.

**Key Finding:** Specification availability is highly variable. While design features and test results are often published, critical mission planning data (mass, power, cost) is rarely disclosed publicly. Only 2/10 systems have mass specifications, 0/10 have power requirements, and 1/10 has cost data.

**Market Landscape:** US leads in inflatable technology (Sierra Space, Lockheed Martin) and 3D printing (ICON, AI SpaceFactory). European vendors (Thales Alenia Space) dominate rigid pressurized modules. Supply chain shows concentration risk (ILC Dover exclusive to Sierra Space, Vectran from single Japanese manufacturer).

## Vendors/Systems Documented (10 total)

### Inflatable/Expandable Modules (4 systems)

1. **Sierra Space - LIFE Habitat**
   - TRL: 6-7 (ground testing complete)
   - Volume: 300-5,000 m³ (variants)
   - Crew: 4-6
   - Status: Most advanced US inflatable, burst tests successful
   - Gap: Mass, power, cost not disclosed

2. **Lockheed Martin - Lunar Inflatable Habitat**
   - TRL: 6 (lunar-specific design)
   - Volume: 250 m³
   - Floor Area: 50 m²
   - Status: Purpose-designed for lunar surface, in-space demo ~2029-2030
   - Gap: Mass, power, cost, crew capacity not disclosed

3. **Max Space - Max Space 20**
   - TRL: 6 (flight 2025)
   - Volume: 2 m³ → 20 m³ (10:1 compression)
   - Status: Bigelow heritage, largest expandable to be flown
   - Roadmap: 1,000 m³ by 2030
   - Gap: Mass, power, cost, crew capacity not disclosed

4. **ILC Dover - Inflatable Structures**
   - TRL: 7 (partner to Sierra Space)
   - Role: Exclusive softgoods supplier to Sierra Space LIFE
   - Historical: X-Hab, InFlex, Toroidal, Expandable Lunar Habitat (NASA projects)
   - Status: Component supplier, not prime contractor
   - Note: Supply chain single point of failure

### Rigid Pressurized Modules (3 systems)

5. **Northrop Grumman - HALO**
   - TRL: 8-9 (in production)
   - Application: Lunar Gateway ORBITAL (not surface)
   - Mass: 8,500 kg
   - Dimensions: 7m x 3m diameter
   - Crew: 4 for 30+ days
   - Cost: $1.1B (NASA contract)
   - Launch: 2027, Falcon Heavy
   - Status: Most mature system, Cygnus heritage
   - Note: Rigid, not inflatable

6. **Thales Alenia Space - Multi-Purpose Habitat (MPH)**
   - TRL: 5-6 (design phase)
   - Application: Lunar surface (permanent habitat)
   - Mass: 15,000 kg
   - Dimensions: 6m x 3m
   - Crew: 2 for 7-30 days
   - Launch: 2033
   - Status: First permanent lunar surface rigid habitat, mobile
   - Partnership: ASI (Italy) / NASA Artemis Accords
   - Gap: Power, cost not disclosed

7. **Thales Alenia Space - Lunar I-Hab**
   - TRL: 7-8 (in development)
   - Application: Lunar Gateway ORBITAL (not surface)
   - Volume: ~10 m³
   - Crew: 4 for up to 90 days (with HALO)
   - Status: ESA prime, JAXA contributions (life support)
   - Partnership: ESA/JAXA collaboration
   - Gap: Mass, power, cost not disclosed

### 3D Printed In-Situ Construction (2 systems)

8. **ICON - Project Olympus**
   - TRL: 5-6 (ground testing)
   - Technology: Laser melts regolith → ceramic structures
   - Contract: $57.2M NASA SBIR Phase III through 2028
   - Applications: Landing pads, roads, habitats
   - Testing: NASA Marshall, lunar simulant + actual regolith
   - Earth Analog: Mars Dune Alpha (1,700 sq ft at JSC)
   - Status: Most funded 3D printing project
   - Gap: System mass, power, habitat specs scalable/not disclosed

9. **AI SpaceFactory - LINA**
   - TRL: 4-5 (early development)
   - Technology: Autonomous robotic 3D printing, regolith-polymer
   - Design: Vertical egg-shape, 4 floors, dual-shell thermal
   - Testing: Vacuum chamber at -200°C
   - Awards: $500K NASA 3D Printed Habitat Challenge (1st place, MARSHA)
   - Partnership: NASA Kennedy Space Center ACO program
   - Status: Earlier TRL than ICON, NASA award winner
   - Gap: Mass, volume, power, cost not disclosed

### Life Support Integration (1 system)

10. **Collins Aerospace - ECLSS**
    - TRL: 8-9 (ISS heritage operational, lunar variants 7-8)
    - Heritage: 50+ years, Apollo origins
    - Capabilities: Water processing, CO2 reduction, O2 generation (regenerative)
    - Configuration: Modular pallets, 72" x 30.19"
    - Integration: 3 pallets for Air Revitalization in NASA Surface Habitat design
    - Current: ISS spacesuits, Orion ECLSS
    - Status: Most mature life support technology
    - Gap: Mass, power, cost not disclosed

## Key Insights

### 1. Orbital vs. Surface Habitat Distinction
- **Orbital (Gateway):** HALO, I-Hab - mature, in production, NOT lunar surface
- **Lunar Surface:** Lockheed Martin, Thales MPH, ICON, AI SpaceFactory - purpose-designed
- **Dual-Use Potential:** Sierra Space LIFE, Max Space - designed for orbit but adaptable

### 2. Inflatable vs. Rigid Trade-offs
| Factor | Inflatable | Rigid |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Launch Volume Efficiency | Excellent (5-10:1 compression) | Poor (no compression) |
| Mass Data Availability | Poor (not disclosed) | Better (2/3 disclosed) |
| Puncture Risk | Higher | Lower |
| TRL | 6-7 (testing phase) | 7-9 (production) |
| Heritage | Limited (Bigelow BEAM) | Extensive (ISS, Cygnus) |

### 3. International Collaboration Significant
- **US:** Leading inflatable tech and 3D printing
- **Europe (Italy/ESA):** Leading rigid pressurized modules (Thales)
- **Japan (JAXA):** Life support contributions, Vectran material supplier
- **Absent:** Russia (geopolitical), China (language barrier), India (status unknown)

### 4. Supply Chain Concentration Risks
- **ILC Dover:** Exclusive to Sierra Space LIFE - single point of failure for US inflatable softgoods
- **Vectran:** Single supplier (Kuraray, Japan) - material dependency
- **Thales Alenia Space:** Supplier to multiple programs (HALO, I-Hab, MPH) - concentration but also expertise

### 5. Cost Opacity Significant Challenge
- Only HALO has transparent costs ($1.1B full NASA contract)
- Commercial vendors reluctant to publish pricing pre-availability
- ICON $57.2M is development funding, not per-unit cost
- Cost estimates will require indirect methods (analogous systems, contract values, technical papers)

### 6. Power Requirements Universal Gap
- 0/10 vendors disclose power consumption
- Critical for lunar surface mission planning (limited solar, nuclear considerations)
- ECLSS continuous power especially important
- Will require NASA technical documents or engineering papers

### 7. 3D Printing Most Promising for Large Infrastructure
- Eliminates launch mass constraints
- Best radiation/thermal/micrometeorite protection
- Lower TRL (4-6) requires more development
- Two approaches: ICON (laser sintering) vs. AI SpaceFactory (regolith-polymer extrusion)
