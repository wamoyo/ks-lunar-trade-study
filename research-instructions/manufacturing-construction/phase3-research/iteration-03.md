## Search Strategy 3: Power Requirements Literature (CRITICAL METRIC GAP)

### General Power Requirements for Regolith Processing

**Conventional Thermal Sintering:**
- **233 kWh** to heat 1 ton of lunar regolith to 1000°C (neglecting system losses)
- **5.73 kWh** for sample-scale processing
- Equivalent to 100 m² photovoltaic array operating 6 hours during lunar day

**Cold Sintering Process (CSP):**
- **0.86 kWh** for sample-scale (6.66x lower than conventional thermal sintering)
- Energy-efficient alternative

**Microwave Sintering:**
- **69-98 MJ/kg** (approximately **19-27 kWh/kg**)
- High energy consumption

**Direct Laser Sintering:**
- **40 W laser output** sufficient to melt regolith-like materials (small scale)
- Could be powered by ~0.5 m² solar panels

**Large-Scale Applications:**
- **~400 kW nuclear power plant** suggested for radiation shielding construction

**Sources:**
- [Energy Requirements for ISRU Radiation Shield](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0273117720301708)
- [Cold Sintering Strategy](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0264127524000467)
- [Microwave Sintering Energy](https://ascelibrary.org/doi/10.1061/(ASCE)0893-1321(2005)18:3(188))
- [Sintering Energy Comparison](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0955221924009208)

---

### Laser Melting Power Specifications

**ESA PAVER Project (Large-Scale):**
- **12 kW CO2 laser** maximum power
- **10 kW** selected for manufacturing 2D geometries
- Spot diameter up to 100 mm
- Laser intensity: **142 W/cm²**
- Surface temperatures: **1600°C**

**MOONRISE Payload (Mobile Platform):**
- **30-120 W** output power range
- **70W** baseline for 6 seconds
- **300 W** maximum total energy (brief operation)

**Laboratory-Scale Studies:**
- **50-145 W** laser power for dense parts without macroscopic defects
- **60-100 W** range for successful regolith melting
- **145 W** optimal for powder bed fusion with 390 mm/s scan speed

**Sources:**
- [Laser Melting Large Elements](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-42008-1)
- [MOONRISE Payload](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9831544/)
- [Laser Melting Parameters](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/268037920_Process_Parameters_Development_of_Selective_Laser_Melting_of_Lunar_Regolith_for_On-Site_Manufacturing_Applications)

---

### Power Requirements Summary

**CRITICAL FINDING:** Power requirements are **THE** major constraint for lunar construction systems.

**Scale Dependencies:**
- Small-scale laser systems: 50-300 W
- Mobile laser systems: 100-500 W
- Large-scale laser systems: 10-12 kW
- Conventional sintering: 5-30 kWh per operation cycle
- Industrial-scale construction: 100-400+ kW

**Implications:**
- Solar power feasible for small-scale operations during lunar day
- Nuclear power likely required for large-scale continuous construction
- Energy storage critical for lunar night operations
- Power availability may limit construction system selection more than technology maturity
