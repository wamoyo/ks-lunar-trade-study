# Phase 2: Search Strategies - Robotics and Robotic Arms

**Date:** 2025-12-08
**Category:** Robotics and Robotic Arms for Lunar Surface Operations
**TRL Focus:** 5-9 (systems in development through flight-proven)

## Overview

Based on Phase 1 preliminary research, this document outlines 8 comprehensive search strategies to ensure complete coverage of commercially available lunar robotics and robotic arms. Each strategy targets different market segments and information sources to minimize the risk of missing vendors or systems.

## Strategy 1: NASA TechPort Systematic Search (REQUIRED)

**Objective:** Identify all NASA-funded robotics projects with TRL 5+ and their commercial transition potential

**Search Method:**
1. Web interface search at https://techport.nasa.gov
2. Use filters:
   - Technology Areas: TX04 (Robotics, Tele-Robotics and Autonomous Systems)
   - TRL: 5, 6, 7, 8, 9
   - Project status: Active and Completed (completed projects may have commercialized)
3. Search terms to try individually and in combination:
   - "lunar robot"
   - "robotic arm"
   - "manipulator"
   - "autonomous"
   - "mobile manipulation"
   - "ISRU"
   - "regolith"
   - "construction"
   - "end effector"

**Data to Extract:**
- Project ID and title
- Lead organization (vendor name)
- TRL (current and target)
- Technology description
- Project status and timeline
- Any technical specifications in description
- Contact information

**Expected Outcome:** 20-50 relevant projects, 10-20 unique vendors

## Strategy 2: Direct Vendor Research

**Objective:** Get product specifications directly from vendor websites and catalogs

**Vendors to Research (from Phase 1):**

**Tier 1 - Confirmed TRL 9 (Space-Proven):**
1. MDA Space (mda.space)
   - Search: Canadarm3, commercial robotics, manipulator arms
   - Look for: Product catalog, specifications, pricing (if available)
   - Check: News/press releases for commercial sales

2. Maxar Technologies (maxar.com)
   - Search: Lanteris Space Systems, SPIDER, LUnA
   - Look for: Robotic systems catalog, Mars heritage products

3. Motiv Space Systems (motivspacesystems.com)
   - Search: Robotic arms, manipulators, heritage products
   - Look for: Product line, specifications, customization options

**Tier 2 - TRL 5-8 (Advanced Development):**
4. GITAI (gitai.tech)
   - Search: All products (IN1, S10, lunar rover, commercial offerings)
   - Check: Availability timeline, 2026 demo mission details

5. Honeybee Robotics / Blue Origin
   - honeybeerow.com / blueorigin.com
   - Search: Robotic arms, end effectors, lunar systems
   - Check: Product catalog, DOF specifications

6. PickNik Robotics (picknik.ai)
   - Search: Space robotics, Artemis products, Space ROS
   - Check: Commercial product availability vs R&D projects

7. Astrobotic Technology (astrobotic.com)
   - Search: LunaGrid, CubeRover, robotic systems
   - Check: Product specifications, integration options

**Data to Extract:**
- Model/variant name
- TRL (if stated)
- Mass (kg)
- Power requirements (kW)
- Reach, payload capacity, DOF
- Cost estimate (if public)
- Turnaround time / availability
- Customization options

**Expected Outcome:** Detailed specs for 15-25 products

## Strategy 3: NASA SBIR/STTR Database Mining

**Objective:** Identify small businesses transitioning from NASA funding to commercial products

**Search Method:**
1. SBIR.gov company search and NASA SBIR/STTR database
2. Focus on:
   - Phase II recipients (closer to commercial viability)
   - Phase III (commercial transition phase)
   - Focus areas: Robotics, autonomous systems, manipulation, ISRU

**Search Terms:**
- "lunar robot"
- "robotic arm"
- "autonomous manipulation"
- "regolith handling"
- "space robotics"
- "end effector"

**Companies to Track:**
- Phase II → check if commercial product exists now
- Phase I recipients from 2020-2024 → check for Phase II progression
- Look for press releases announcing commercial products

**Data Sources:**
- sbir.gov
- sbir.nasa.gov
- NASA SBIR Firms Library
- Company websites of SBIR recipients

**Expected Outcome:** 10-15 additional vendors, some may overlap with Strategy 2

## Strategy 6: Reverse Engineering from Known Missions

**Objective:** Identify vendors by tracking who supplies robotics for confirmed lunar missions

**Missions to Research:**

**Artemis Program:**
- Gateway robotic systems (Canadarm3 = MDA already identified)
- HLS (Human Landing System) providers → check for robotic systems
- Artemis surface payloads with robotic components

**Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS):**
- Intuitive Machines missions → robotic payloads
- Astrobotic missions → robotic systems (already identified)
- Firefly Aerospace missions → Honeybee rover (already identified)
- Draper missions

**Commercial Space Stations:**
- Axiom Station → MDA robotics (already identified, confirms market)
- Starlab → check for robotic arm suppliers
- Orbital Reef (Blue Origin/Sierra Space) → likely Honeybee

**International Missions:**
- Chang'e missions (China) → commercial suppliers? (likely government-only)
- Luna missions (Russia) → commercial suppliers? (likely government-only)
- Chandrayaan (India) → check for commercial components

**Search Method:**
- Mission websites and NASA mission pages
- Payload manifests
- Prime contractor press releases
- Subcontractor announcements

**Expected Outcome:** Validate existing vendors, possibly 2-5 new vendors

## Strategy 7: Adjacent Industry Crossover

**Objective:** Identify terrestrial robotics companies offering space-qualified variants

**Industries to Search:**

**Industrial Robotics:**
- Companies: ABB, KUKA, Fanuc, Yaskawa, Universal Robots
- Search: "space robotics", "space-qualified", "vacuum rated"
- Check: Special products divisions, space partnerships

**Defense Contractors:**
- General Dynamics, Northrop Grumman, Lockheed Martin, Boeing
- Search: Space robotics divisions, lunar systems
- Check: May have separate commercial offerings from government contracts

**Subsea/Underwater Robotics:**
- Similar environment challenges (pressure, extreme conditions)
- Companies with space crossover programs
- Example: Atmospheric diving suit companies → space suit/robotic connections

**Nuclear Industry:**
- Remote manipulation experience (radiation environments)
- Space applications for robots in harsh environments

**Search Method:**
- Company websites → space divisions
- LinkedIn: company pages filtered for "space" keywords
- Trade publications for announcements
- Patent searches for space applications

**Expected Outcome:** 3-8 vendors, likely specialized/niche products

## Strategy 8: Technical Literature and Patent Search

**Objective:** Identify emerging technologies and vendors in academic/patent literature

**Sources:**

**Academic Databases:**
- IEEE Xplore: "lunar robotics" + "TRL" or "flight test"
- Frontiers in Robotics and AI (open access)
- Journal of Field Robotics
- Look for: Authors' affiliations → companies commercializing research

**NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS):**
- Search: "lunar robot", "robotic arm", "manipulation"
- Filter: Recent publications (2020-2025)
- Look for: Industry partner acknowledgments

**Patent Databases:**
- NASA Technology Transfer Program patents available for licensing
- Google Patents: "lunar robot", "space manipulator", filter by recent
- Look for: Assignees = companies who might license or have products

**ArXiv:**
- Robotics section
- Search lunar/space applications
- Track which companies authors represent

**Expected Outcome:** 5-10 emerging vendors, some very early stage (exclude if <TRL 5)
